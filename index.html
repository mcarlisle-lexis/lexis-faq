Before You Arrive

8 questions
What documents do I need to bring to Australia? ▼

Please bring the following documents with you:

  • Valid passport — ensure it is valid for the full duration of your stay
  • Student visa grant letter — keep a printed and digital copy
  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) — provided by Lexis English
  • Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) policy — required for all student visa holders
  • Evidence of funds — bank statements or scholarship letters
  • Emergency contact details — family member or trusted person back home
  • Accommodation confirmation — letter or booking reference for your first night
Keep digital copies of all documents in your email or cloud storage in case originals are lost.
What is OSHC and do I need it? ▼

Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is compulsory health insurance for all international students in Australia. It is a visa requirement — you cannot enter Australia on a student visa without it.

Lexis English arranges OSHC through Allianz Care Australia on your behalf. Your OSHC covers:

  • Doctor visits (GP)
  • Hospital treatment
  • Some prescription medicines
  • Emergency ambulance

For claims or queries, contact Allianz OSHC: 1300 136 742 or visit allianzcare.com.au

OSHC does not cover dental, optical, or physiotherapy. Consider additional private insurance for these services.
When should I arrive in Australia? ▼

We recommend arriving at least 2–3 days before your course start date. This gives you time to:

  • Settle into your accommodation
  • Buy essential items (bedding, toiletries, groceries)
  • Get a local SIM card
  • Rest and recover from jet lag
  • Explore the local area

Your First Day (Orientation) is the official start of your Lexis English program. Placement testing begins at approximately 8:30am–9:00am. Please arrive on time.

How do I get a local SIM card? ▼

You can purchase a prepaid SIM card from convenience stores, supermarkets (Coles, Woolworths), or phone carrier stores. Popular providers for international students include:

  • Optus — good coverage nationwide
  • Telstra — best regional coverage
  • Vodafone — competitive international call rates
  • Aldi Mobile / Boost — affordable prepaid plans

You will need your passport to register a SIM card in Australia. Plans typically start from $10–$30/month.

What should I pack? ▼

Australia has a varied climate depending on location and season. General packing recommendations:

  • Noosa / Sunshine Coast / Brisbane / Byron Bay: Warm and subtropical — light clothing year-round, bring a light jacket for winter (June–August)
  • Sydney (Manly): Mild — pack layers for cooler months, bring sun protection
  • Perth City: Hot, dry summers; mild winters — sun protection is essential

Always bring: sunscreen (SPF 50+), a hat, and sunglasses — Australian UV is intense all year.

Can I open an Australian bank account before I arrive? ▼

Yes — major Australian banks allow you to apply for an account online before arrival. The most popular banks for international students are:

  • Commonwealth Bank (CBA) — Westfield Smart Access account, popular with students
  • ANZ — Access Advantage account
  • Westpac — Choice account
  • NAB — Classic Banking account

You will need to visit a branch within 6 weeks of arrival to verify your identity with your passport. Bring your passport, CoE, and Australian address.

Avoid carrying large amounts of cash. Set up your account early so your card is ready when you arrive.
How do I get from the airport to my campus? ▼

Transport options vary by campus:

  • Noosa: Brisbane Airport approx. 75 min; Sunshine Coast (Maroochydore) Airport approx. 30 min
  • Sunshine Coast: Maroochydore Airport approx. 15–20 min by taxi (~$25); from Brisbane Airport (~60 min) via Col's Air Shuttle or Sunair Bus — ask Reception for timetables
  • Brisbane: Airtrain ~$22 | Taxi ~$50–60 | Bus ~$20
  • Byron Bay: Gold Coast Airport ~45 min (~$35 taxi) | Brisbane Airport ~2 hrs (~$65)
  • Sydney (Manly): Train + Ferry ~$25–35 | Taxi ~$90–120
  • Perth City: Bus ~$5 | Taxi/Uber ~$45–60 | Airport Shuttle ~$25–35

We recommend booking a taxi, rideshare (Uber/DiDi), or pre-arranged shuttle for your first arrival.

Can I bring my prescription medication to Australia? ▼

Yes, but there are important rules:

  • Bring a doctor's letter in English confirming your condition and medication
  • Keep medication in its original labelled packaging
  • Bring enough supply for your stay, or check if it is available in Australia
  • Some medications are controlled substances in Australia — check with the Australian Border Force before travelling

Visit www.tga.gov.au for the Australian Therapeutic Goods Administration's traveller information.

Your first day is a structured orientation to help you settle in. The general schedule is:

Exact times may vary slightly by campus. Your campus will confirm the full schedule with you.
What is the placement test and how does it work? ▼

The placement test assesses your current English level so we can place you in the right class. It consists of:

  • Written test — grammar and vocabulary (approximately 90 minutes)
  • Speaking interview — one-on-one conversation with a teacher (approximately 10–15 minutes)

Results are used to place you in one of six levels: Beginner (A1), Elementary (A2), Pre-Intermediate (B1), Intermediate (B1+), Upper Intermediate (B2), or Advanced (C1).

Do not study the night before — we need to accurately assess your current level, not what you can memorise.

  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)
  • OSHC card or policy documents
  • A pen
  • Notepad or exercise book
  • Australian phone number (if you have one)
  • Local address where you are staying

Bring a light lunch or some money — there are cafes and shops nearby most campuses.

Will I get a student card? ▼

Yes! You will receive a Lexis English Student Card which provides discounts at local businesses, cafes, and tourist attractions.

You will also be introduced to the My Lexis App — your digital hub for timetables, learning resources, activity bookings, and campus news.

Academic Program

6 questions
How many hours per week will I study? ▼

The General English (GE) program runs 20 lessons per week (Monday–Friday). Each lesson is 45 minutes, giving approximately 15 hours of classroom instruction per week.

Additional elective activities include:

  • Power Up sessions — skills workshops (pronunciation, exam preparation, conversation clubs)
  • Homework Help — after-class support with teachers
  • Social programme — excursions, sport, beach days, cultural events
What English levels does Lexis offer? ▼

We offer six levels aligned to the Common European Framework (CEFR):

  • Beginner — A1
  • Elementary — A2
  • Pre-Intermediate — B1
  • Intermediate — B1+
  • Upper Intermediate — B2
  • Advanced — C1
Byron Bay campus does not offer Beginner level. If you are a complete beginner, please contact us to discuss the right campus for you.
What is the attendance requirement? ▼

All students must maintain a minimum attendance of 80% in each four-week study period. This is both a Lexis English requirement and a student visa condition.

If your attendance falls below 80%:

  • You will receive a formal written warning
  • Continued non-attendance may result in visa reporting to the Department of Home Affairs (DHA)
  • In serious cases, your enrolment may be cancelled
If you are sick, contact your campus immediately. Medical absences may be considered separately if you provide a doctor's certificate.
How do I move up to the next level? ▼

Level advancement is assessed at the end of each study period. To advance to the next level you must:

  • Complete a level progression test
  • Achieve the required score for your current level
  • Maintain minimum 80% attendance

Your teacher will advise you if you are ready to advance. If you do not pass, you will continue in the same level with new course content.

Byron Bay campus requires 80% attendance specifically for level advancement.
Do I need to buy textbooks? ▼

Lexis English uses Cambridge Empower series textbooks. Your course materials are included in your tuition fee — you do not need to purchase textbooks separately.

You will be issued your textbook on or after your first day of classes. Please bring it to every lesson and take care of it.

Do I have to speak English at school? ▼

Yes — Lexis English operates an English Only Policy on all campuses. This applies in classrooms, common areas, and during school activities.

Speaking English at all times — even with friends from your home country — is the fastest way to improve. Immersion works!

This policy applies to students. Staff and teachers may use other languages when necessary to support student welfare.

What is homestay like? ▼

Homestay means living with an approved Australian host family. It is an excellent option for students who want to practise English at home and experience Australian family life.

Typically included:

  • Private bedroom
  • Breakfast and dinner (varies by arrangement)
  • Access to shared bathroom, laundry, and kitchen
  • Wi-Fi

All Lexis homestay families are vetted and approved. There is a Homestay Code of Conduct for both students and host families to ensure a positive experience.

What is a property inspection and why does it matter? ▼

When you move into rental accommodation, you should complete a Property Condition Report (inspection checklist) within the first 3 days. This documents:

  • Any existing damage to walls, floors, fixtures, appliances
  • Condition of furniture and fittings

This protects you from being held responsible for pre-existing damage when you leave. Keep a copy and send one to the agent or landlord.

Lexis English provides a Property Inspection Checklist template in Appendix A of your Pre-Arrival Handbook.
What is a rental bond? ▼

A bond is a security deposit paid at the start of a tenancy — typically 4 weeks' rent. In Australia, bonds are lodged with a government authority (not kept by the landlord):

  • QLD: Residential Tenancies Authority (RTA)
  • NSW: NSW Fair Trading
  • WA: Bond Administrator (Department of Commerce)

Your bond is returned at the end of your tenancy if the property is left in the same condition as when you moved in (allowing for fair wear and tear).

Working in Australia

5 questions
Can I work while studying in Australia? ▼

Yes — student visa holders are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight (approximately 24 hours per week on average) while their course is in session.

There is no limit on working hours during school holidays or official vacation periods.

Working more than the permitted hours is a visa breach and can result in cancellation of your student visa. Do not exceed your work entitlements.
How do I get a Tax File Number (TFN)? ▼

A Tax File Number (TFN) is a unique number issued by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). You need one to work legally and to pay the correct amount of tax.

To apply:

  • Visit www.ato.gov.au and complete the online TFN application
  • You must be in Australia and have an Australian address
  • Your TFN will arrive by post within 28 days

Give your TFN to your employer when you start work. Without it, you will be taxed at the highest rate (47%).

ATO Helpline: 13 28 61

What is superannuation? ▼

Superannuation (super) is Australia's compulsory retirement savings system. Your employer must pay an additional 11.5% of your ordinary earnings into a super fund on your behalf (as of FY2024–25).

You choose a super fund — many banks and financial institutions offer them. When you permanently leave Australia, you may be able to claim your super back through the Departing Australia Superannuation Payment (DASP) scheme.

To claim DASP after leaving: visit www.ato.gov.au/dasp

Make sure your employer is actually paying your super — check your super fund statements regularly.
Do I need to lodge a tax return? ▼

If you worked in Australia during a financial year (1 July – 30 June), you should lodge a tax return by 31 October each year. You may be entitled to a refund if too much tax was withheld from your wages.

You can lodge online at myTax via ato.gov.au or use a registered tax agent. Many tax agents offer free or low-cost returns for students.

What are my rights as a worker in Australia? ▼

All workers in Australia — including international students — are protected by the Fair Work Act. Your rights include:

  • National minimum wage (currently $24.10/hr as of July 2024)
  • Penalty rates for weekends, public holidays, and overtime
  • Safe working conditions
  • Protection from unfair dismissal
  • Right to join a union

If you believe you are being underpaid or treated unfairly, contact the Fair Work Ombudsman: 13 13 94 or www.fairwork.gov.au

You have rights regardless of your visa status. Do not be afraid to report exploitative employers.

Money & Cost of Living

4 questions
How much money do I need per week? ▼

Estimated weekly costs (excluding accommodation) — 2025 figures:

  • Groceries/food: $100–$150/week (cooking at home)
  • Eating out: $15–$25 per meal at a café/restaurant
  • Public transport: $30–$60/week depending on city
  • Phone/internet: $15–$30/month
  • Entertainment/activities: $50–$100/week
  • Total (excl. rent): approximately $300–$500/week

Combined with rent, budget at least $700–$900/week in major cities (Sydney, Brisbane), or $600–$750 in regional campuses (Noosa, Sunshine Coast).

Are there ATM fees in Australia? ▼

Most major Australian banks have removed ATM fees for their own customers. However:

  • Using another bank's ATM may incur a fee (typically $2–$3)
  • Using an overseas bank card will incur international transaction fees (typically 2–3%)
  • Eftpos (direct card payment) is free at most shops and supermarkets

Opening an Australian bank account quickly will save you significant fees over a long stay.

How do I send money back home? ▼

You can send money internationally using:

  • Wise (formerly TransferWise) — low fees, good exchange rates
  • Western Union / MoneyGram — available at post offices and newsagencies
  • Your Australian bank — international transfer, may have higher fees
  • PayPal — convenient but check exchange rates

Always compare exchange rates and fees before transferring. Wise typically offers the best rates for student amounts.

What are my consumer rights in Australia? ▼

Australian consumer law gives you strong rights when buying goods and services:

  • Refund or replacement for faulty goods — even without a receipt if you can prove purchase
  • Goods must be fit for purpose and match descriptions
  • Services must be provided with reasonable care and skill

For disputes, contact the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC): www.accc.gov.au

Health & Safety

5 questions
What should I do if I get sick? ▼

If you are too sick to attend class:

  • Call your campus immediately before classes begin to report your absence
  • Visit a GP (General Practitioner) for a medical certificate if you are absent more than 1 day
  • Submit the medical certificate to student services — medical absences may be excluded from your attendance record

Your OSHC card covers visits to bulk-billing GPs. Ask at reception if the doctor bulk bills — this means no out-of-pocket cost for you.

Do not come to school if you have COVID-19 symptoms. Contact the campus by phone.
Is there mental health support available? ▼

Yes — Lexis English has a Student Support Officer at each campus who can provide pastoral care and refer you to professional support services if needed.

National mental health resources:

  • Lifeline — 13 11 14 (24/7 crisis support)
  • Beyond Blue — 1300 22 4636 | beyondblue.org.au
  • Kids Helpline (under 25) — 1800 55 1800

Speak to your Student Services coordinator — everything you share is treated with confidentiality.

How do I stay safe at the beach? ▼

Australian beaches can have dangerous rip currents. Always follow these rules:

  • Always swim between the red and yellow flags — this is the patrolled area
  • Never swim alone
  • If caught in a rip: don't fight it — float, raise your arm, and wait for rescue OR swim parallel to shore to escape
  • Check conditions before entering — look for signs and ask lifeguards
  • Never swim at unpatrolled beaches, at night, or when flags are not flying
Many international students drown in Australia each year. Rip currents can affect even strong swimmers. When in doubt, stay out.
How do I protect myself from the Australian sun? ▼

Australia has one of the highest UV radiation levels in the world. Protect yourself with the "Slip, Slop, Slap, Seek, Slide" method:

  • Slip on sun-protective clothing
  • Slop on SPF 50+ sunscreen (reapply every 2 hours)
  • Slap on a hat (broad brim)
  • Seek shade between 10am and 3pm
  • Slide on sunglasses (UV400)

You can get sunburnt even on cloudy days. Don't underestimate Australian UV.

Is it safe to walk alone at night? ▼

Australia is generally safe, but follow common-sense precautions at night:

  • Travel with a friend where possible
  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas
  • Avoid displaying expensive items (phone, jewellery, laptop)
  • Let someone know where you are going and when you expect to return
  • Use registered taxis or Uber rather than accepting rides from strangers
  • Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, seek help immediately
If you feel unsafe, call 000 immediately. You can also call the Lexis English 24-hour emergency number for your campus.

Visa & Legal

4 questions
What are my student visa conditions? ▼

As a student visa (Subclass 500) holder, you must:

  • Maintain enrolment at a registered CRICOS provider
  • Attend classes — minimum 80% attendance
  • Maintain satisfactory course progress
  • Work no more than 48 hours per fortnight during study periods
  • Maintain OSHC for the duration of your visa
  • Notify your education provider of address changes within 7 days

Breaching these conditions can result in your visa being cancelled. Contact the Department of Home Affairs (DHA) if you have visa questions: 13 1881

Can I extend my course and stay longer? ▼

Yes — you can extend your enrolment at Lexis English. Speak to your Student Services coordinator about extending your course. They will issue a new Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE).

To extend your student visa, you must apply online through the Department of Home Affairs before your current visa expires: immi.homeaffairs.gov.au

Apply for your visa extension well in advance — processing can take several weeks. Do not overstay your visa.
Can I get a refund if I cancel my course? ▼

Lexis English's refund policy depends on when you cancel and the reason:

  • Visa refused before course starts: full refund minus an administration fee
  • Cancellation before course starts (non-visa): partial refund — enrolment fee and notice period deducted
  • Cancellation after course begins: refund for unused complete weeks, minus applicable fees
  • Lexis cancels the course: full refund of unused fees
